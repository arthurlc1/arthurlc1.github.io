  • Sarah Espiritt

    Geography

    I am a second year Law student at Bristol University. I can offer tuition in Geography, English Literature and Psychology.

    Biography

    I achieved A*A*A (Geography, Psychology and English Literature) in my A-levels. I attended a state school which sent very few people (3 in my year) to Russell Group universities so I am a very motivated person as well as being a strong proponent of the mantra that anything is possible, regardless of your background. This is the work ethic I will strive to encourage as a tutor. I am currently a mentor for year twelve and thirteen students wishing to study law at university and part of my role as mentor also has me assisting them in their revision given that I studied some of the A-levels they are currently doing.

    Personal Approach

    I will prepare my lessons in accordance to each student’s unique requirements and working techniques, whether that be learning content ahead of what is being taught in school or focusing on a topic which is of a particular difficulty. I appreciate that people work in different ways; I myself am a very visual learner so I make sure that my notes are relatable to pictures which I can remember when sitting exams. I will use the opportunity I have been given as a tutor to create a comfortable space for each student, in which they can ask questions which they might not have been confident enough to ask in the classroom. Exam technique is a huge part of doing any exam well which is why I will look to develop their exam technique and have each student comfortable and familiar with what is expected of when with more than enough time before sitting their exams.

    When I’m not tutoring…

    When I am not tutoring, I am often planning day trips with friends as I love to travel and explore new places. I am a lover of the outdoors and believe that in order to be successful in your studies, a healthy work life balance is important.

  • Eve Edwards

    History

    Hi, my name is Eve and I am a second year History student at the University of Bristol. I have been tutoring over the past two years in a diverse range of subjects from Maths to English and History, at both KS3 and GCSE level. During my secondary education, my school instilled within me a passion for learning, through their communicative and hands-on approach. This has transgressed to my own tutoring methods, and my key goal is to make learning the most fun and accessible it can be.

    Biography

    I remained at the same school in London from Reception to Year 13, and what I found most important about my experience there was the relationship all of our students developed with the teachers. Not only did the school follow a policy of mutual respect, but the outstanding results they achieve derive from the individual students being able to seek help and advice from their teachers and mentors. This led me to gain 10 A* and A grades at GCSE, an A* A* A in History, Music, and English Literature A-Level, with a B in Maths AS. Although I chose to study History at University, my love for all my previous subjects has not gone away, and I continue to pursue them in my extra curricular life.

    Personal Approach

    In my previous tutoring experience, I found that the most successful and effective methods of teaching is through an engaging programme. I would endeavour to tailor the sessions to the individual, so they can get the most out of their education. To find out the ways in which the student learns, be it through memory tests, debating topics, or a more visual approach to learning. I would hope to transfer my own range of revision techniques that led me to my success, along with other ways of learning to the tutee. Doing so, I hope that we would build a relationship of mutual respect and understanding through which we could tap into and unlock their potential for learning. I believe that what you draw from the individual is of equal importance to what you put in.

    When I'm not tutoring...

    I'm in the music societies at my University, of which I will be creating and leading my own musical ensemble group in the coming year. I also have continued to follow my love of debate, by attending the wide variety of discussions and lectures available to us at UoB. I would classify myself as a people person, and delegate downtime to also socialising and enjoying everything Bristol has to offer with my friends.
