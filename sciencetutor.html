  • Matt Walker

    Chemistry & Biology

    Hello! I am an adaptable, outgoing and dependable second year medical student at the University of Bristol with previous tutoring experience of GCSE age students. My studies have allowed me to deepen my understanding of both biology and chemistry and thus I am able to offer tutoring in these subjects at GCSE level.

    I attended a state school and state sixth form and left with A*s in GCSE biology and chemistry as well as an A and A* at A Level respectively. At sixth form I also received an award the ‘Overall Academic Achievement’ for the entire year group. Science has constituted a large part of my school life and I have therefore cultured a strong passion and enthusiasm for the subject – traits that I believe are necessary in order to tutor successfully and consequently allow students to fulfil their potential.

    Having captained my local football team for 4 years I have developed patience and an ability to motivate individuals towards a common goal. In addition, my previous experience in mentoring younger students has taught me the importance of being able to cater for the individual needs of each pupil. Having very recently left the secondary school education system I have up to date knowledge of the specifications and key tips and tricks required in order to achieve maximum marks. Lessons will be tailored towards the different learning styles of the tutee and will be primarily based around the specification. I am a firm believer in the importance of reinforcing knowledge and thus topics will be continually revisited by exploring examination questions early on, this also helps the student become familiar with the types of questions that are asked. I also feel it is vital for the student to not only be able to answer questions, but to gain an understanding of the principles behind them. This ensures that when faced with some of the more challenging questions, students are able to tackle the problems from different angles, separating them from the majority of candidates.

    Aside from my medical degree, in my free time I enjoy reading, running, cycling and playing competitive football for the medical school men’s team. I also plan to start on teaching first aid when I return to university having successfully completed the first aid examination.
