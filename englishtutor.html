  • Jess Hull

    English

    Biography

    I have much experience working with students of all ages in a teaching environment; whilst at school I led weekly reading sessions with younger students who were struggling to engage with particularly challenging texts. Over summer I enjoyed volunteering at a kids’ camp where I encouraged the children to tackle their weaknesses, demonstrating that they were capable of much more than they had previously thought. When I’m not tutoring I have a keen interest in writing and have worked directly under the editor of the Stockport Mail, as well as contributing to various university publications.

    Personal Approach

    I often find students become disillusioned when studying English or History at school, as the texts are not presented in an engaging way. I like to approach texts from a different angle with my students, finding an aspect that interests or affects them personally – something they can relate to. I always ask my students to identify their strengths and weaknesses, tailoring each session to focus on areas which require most attention. Rather than fact retention, English and History require understanding of an idea or event; for this reason I work to make sure my students understand their subject in depth, and so can feel comfortable and confident at exam time. Exam technique is key, particularly with essay subjects, and hard work is necessary – practice, practice, practice.

    When I’m not tutoring…

    In my spare time I like to work on my drawing and painting. I’m also a member of Amnesty International, a human rights organisation which works to prevent and raise awareness of abuse worldwide.
